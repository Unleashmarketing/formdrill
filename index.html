What is Formdrill?

An innovative solution for a classic manufacturing problem

Formdrill is an innovative solution for creating strong threads in thin-walled materials.

A common problem in manufacturing is how to get reliable threads in thin-walled materials. Conventional methods like weld nuts or threaded inserts have significant drawbacks in terms of time, cost, complexity, and overall quality.

The Formdrill solution provides a simple, proven process:

1

A bushing is created from the parent material using a Formdrill tool, increasing the thread area

2

A Formtap creates strong threads in this bushing without cutting

3

The result is a superior thread connection ready for assembly

A quick introduction to the Formdrill process

The Problem

Conventional methods for threading in thin-walled materials are time-consuming, costly, and often unreliable.

The Solution

With Formdrill, you create a bushing directly from the base material that enables a more stable threaded connection - without weld nuts or additional components.

The Result

Stronger connections, faster production times, lower costs and higher reliability for all your threading applications.

The Formdrill Process

Simple, efficient and precise in just three steps

Drill
Tap
Assemble

Step 1: Drill

The Formdrill tool is used to create a bushing directly from the base material. This unique process displaces material rather than removing it, forming a reinforced area with increased wall thickness.

  • Material thickness is increased by up to 3 times
  • No chips or waste material
  • Creates a perfect foundation for strong threads
Time: 10-15 sec
Tool: Formdrill
RPM: 1000-3000

Step 2: Tap

The Formtap creates threads within the bushing that was formed in step 1. Unlike conventional taps that cut material away, the Formtap forms the threads by displacing material, resulting in stronger threads.

  • Higher strength compared to conventional cutting taps
  • No chips to remove or clean up
  • Thread profile optimized for high load capacity
Time: 10-15 sec
Tool: Formtap
RPM: 200-400

Step 3: Assemble

Once the threads are formed, the connection is ready for assembly. The strengthened bushing and formed threads provide a secure, reliable connection point that can withstand high loads and vibration.

  • Up to 90% cost savings vs. conventional methods
  • Connection strength comparable to welded nuts
  • No additional components required

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about Formdrill

What materials can I use with Formdrill?

+

Formdrill works with various metals and alloys including steel, stainless steel, aluminum, copper, brass, and high-strength alloys. The system is compatible with most metallic materials with a wall thickness of 0.5mm to 4mm.

Do I need special equipment to use Formdrill?

+

No special equipment is required. Formdrill works with standard drill presses, CNC machines, or machining centers. The process only requires the Formdrill tool and corresponding Formtap, which can be used with your existing machinery.

How does Formdrill compare to welded nuts or threaded inserts?

+

Compared to welded nuts or threaded inserts, Formdrill offers significant advantages: up to 90% cost savings, faster production time (seconds instead of minutes), no additional components needed, stronger connection strength, and a cleaner process with no welding slag or chips.

What thread sizes can be created with Formdrill?

+

Formdrill can create thread sizes from M3 to M12 (or equivalent in other standards) depending on the material thickness. Each application requires the appropriate Formdrill tool size and corresponding Formtap. Our technical team can help determine the optimal thread size for your specific application.

How long do Formdrill tools last?

+

Formdrill tools are exceptionally durable. With proper use and cooling, a single tool can typically perform several thousand operations before replacement is needed. The exact lifespan depends on the material being processed, cooling conditions, and operation parameters.
